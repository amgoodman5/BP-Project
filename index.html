BAYPINES

SACO, ME

Custom designed shingle-style beach front home with breathtaking ocean views from living room, dining room, porch, kitchen, bedrooms and patio in quiet and private wooded lot setting.

ABOUT BAYPINES

The idea of naming our home “Baypines” came to us during the design and construction process.  We sought to build a home with historic charm that blended into the natural environment.  As we worked with the architect, builder, and landscaper, we purposed to preserve as much wooded seaside beauty as possible, hoping that the home would “read” as a renovated older home rather than new construction.  We realized our mission had been accomplished when early visitors asked about our renovation process.  

OUR PROPERTY

Baypines stands apart in its seamless marriage to the environment. It is a tree-rimmed, oceanfront home, nestled in a pristine Saco Bay landscape. The house so unified with its natural surroundings that it appears to have sprouted from the dunes...

VINTAGE

The stunning, seaside home has vintage touches that enhance the shingle-style architecture reminiscent of shoreline homes of yesteryear. The yard includes a perennial raised bed, Endless Summer hydrangeas, lilac bushes, lilies of the valley, and peonies.

HISTORICAL

The history of the land’s ownership goes back to one of Saco’s most important patriarchs, fathers, the Deering family, who donated the land for Ferry Beach State Park, and the buildings and land for the Saco Library and Saco Museum. This choice location on Saco Bay was the site of their summer compound.

CRAFTSMANSHIP

The interior of the custom-built home is informed by warm, locally-crafted woodwork. Every room has an ocean view. The bead board main living area, beautifully bright and open, has a high ceiling accented by Ipswich pine molding. The floor plan is flexible and provides ample space for sitting, formal dining, and entertaining. The centerpiece of this space is an authentic fieldstone fireplace with a complementing bluestone mantel.

AMPLE VIEWS

At the south end of the first floor in an open and inviting foyer is a uniquely crafted dramatic staircase leading to the second floor. A storage closet is tucked under the eave of the staircase. The over-sized double hung window on the landing mirrors smaller double hung windows throughout the house. The kitchen includes a "dining bar," gorgeous honed Deer Isle granite counters, and Ipswich pine hand-crafted cabinets. Washer and dryer are in a pocket door accessed pantry that is directly off the kitchen.

HOSPITABLE

A hallway leading from the first floor living space to the garage encompasses a guest suite. The guest room closet has built-in shelves, and the adjacent bath has a vintage sink with hand-crafted retro vanity, shower, and subway tile.

PRIVACY

The second floor is spacious and welcoming. The master bedroom has a walk-in closet and en suite bead board bath with antique sink and claw foot tub. This floor contains three other bedrooms, one currently being used as a study, as well as a double sink bathroom. An ocean- facing balcony is accessed from the master bedroom. One of the bedrooms has a built-in loft complete with "seaman's" ladder.

WOOD INSIDE & OUT

Kitchen cabinetry and all wood trim are pine. The stairs to the second level are oak, as are hardwood floors throughout the home.

MODERN AMENITIES

Also noteworthy:  the home has five-zoned propane gas heat, and the basement includes a sauna, shower, exercise space, and game room. The seaside home is protected by a newly rebuilt (professionally installed in October 2014) seawall constructed of marine-grade steel with a hundred year life span
